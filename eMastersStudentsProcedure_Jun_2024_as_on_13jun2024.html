Procedure for Students
1. The student interested in registering for the ODL placement process for the upcoming
placement session must prepare a Master Resume and submit it by the specified
deadline to the Office of Digital Learning (ODL) during registration of placement. All
the proofs justifying each point in the student's master resume need to be submitted
along with the Master Resume.
2. The student is also supposed to submit a short resume in specified standard format
of two pages to ODL to prepare the student profile booklet.
3. The student must register for the ODL placement process within the specified
timeframe as notified.
4. Each program should appoint a team of students as a point of contact (POC) from the
students' side for resume verification and for supporting placement. A team
consisting of minimum of two Department Point of Contacts (DPOC), two Industrial
Coordinators (IC) and one Social Media Coordinator (SMC) for each programme is
necessary to carry on placement activities. You may choose the team independently
or individuals can fill the “Supportive Involvement Towards eMasters Placement
Procedure” (https://forms.gle/794AUy1WVnUqnsGn7) to form the team. This form
has the details of the responsibilities for these roles. Following the ODL placement
team's evaluation of your participation, you will receive a certificate for the same. The
certificates for DPOC may be collected from ODL office when the student comes for
the convocation or will be posted to the address shared by them post-convocation.
The certificates for IC and SMC would be mailed to the students when the students
step down from the post or six months post-graduation, following a review of their
performance by ODL. A minimum of 6 months of association with ODL for a post
is necessary to avail a certificate.
5. Once the name(s) of the POC and the team is decided, ODL will inform the student
coordinators and all the students of the respective programme about the details of all
the team members. It is suggested to build your connections with your senior and
junior batches for smooth handover of responsibilities from one cohort to another.
6. The outgoing DPOC, at least one month preceding their graduation, is responsible for
selecting a successor and notifying the ODL before applying for institute no-dues
clearance. Facilitating a seamless transition of DPOC responsibilities to the newly
appointed individual is a key duty of the outgoing DPOC, and it is a prerequisite for
obtaining clearance of Institute no dues. If the IC or SMC plans to step down from their
position, they must notify ODL at least one month in advance to allow time for a new
IC/SMC to be appointed.
7. The student would be assigned a time window during which the allotted DPOC would
verify the resume.
8. Once the resume has been verified, it should be informed to the ODL. After getting
approval from the DL office, the students need to submit their application through
ODL before the company's deadline for which they are eligible/interested.
9. In case any placement-registered students apply to a company independently, they
would keep ODL informed, especially if they get selected.
10. Students participating in the lateral placement process (facilitated through
ODL/PCs/eMasters Peers), should keep ODL always in the loop, for interviews and
any other communications with the companies.
11. Following the application, the student must attend all of the company's hiring
events (Pre-Placement Talks / Tests / GDs, etc.).
12. When a student receives a job offer from a company, either through ODL placement
or independently, within a year of registering for placement, the ODL office will
promptly notify and initiate automatic de-registration from ODL placement for that
student.
