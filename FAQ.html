    Program Delivery




    Each module will consist of synchronous and asynchronous learning components. Asynchronous learning including recorded videos, reading material, etc will be shared during the weekdays; whereas synchronous learning via live interactive sessions will be conducted during weekends.

    Live interactive sessions will be mostly held during the weekends. The exact schedule for the live interactive sessions for each module will be announced closer to the start of the module.

    There will be 1 hour of live interaction per module, and recorded lectures lasting 1.5-2 hours per week. In total, there will be 20-24 contact hours, including live interactive sessions and recorded lectures, per module per quarter.

    Final exams for each module will be conducted online, then these will be proctored remotely. Exact details about the final exams will be shared closer to the start of the respective modules.

    The exams are under the preview of the instructor. eMasters modules will have continuous evaluation throughout the quarter (assignment, quizzes, presentations etc.; milestones for projects) in virtual mode. Exams may include multiple sections, which may include objective as well as subjective sessions, with or without negative marking. The final exam will be conducted in physical mode.

    The attendance policy may vary from one module to the other and will be enforced by the respective instructor. For genuine cases, instructors may allow absence provided prior intimation (for planned events) or some reason (in case of emergencies) is provided.

    Curriculum and Evaluation




    Each teaching unit in a quarter is defined as a module. A module entails 8 weeks of teaching followed by an examination period of 2 weeks. Each Module will carry 5 credits.

    There are two types of modules- core and electives. A program may have a set of core modules defined that are compulsory and a set of modules as electives. A student must take all core modules and obtain a grade other than 'F' or 'E'. In addition, a student must take sufficient electives to fulfill the credit requirements for the program. For courses requiring pre-announced laboratory components, students may be required to be present on campus for a predefined period.

    The minimum duration for the program is 4 quarters, and the maximum duration will be 12 quarters. The normal load per quarter is 15 credits, where each Module is 5 credits. Each module will be of 10 weeks’ duration: the first 8 weeks will be used for teaching and exams will be held in 9/10 weeks.

    A student may opt for 0, 5, 10 or 15 credits in a quarter, depending on his/her professional workload. As long as they have registered for the program and paid the fee for each quarter, there is no minimum load requirement. The academic authorities may permit overload up to a maximum of 4 modules in exceptional circumstances.

    A student should have completed 60 credits, earned a minimum CPI of 6, paid all the dues to the Institute and should have no pending cases of indiscipline against him/her to successfully graduate from the eMasters Program.

    The module evaluation policies (attendance, grading, marks distribution etc.) will be announced prior to the commencement of a module in that particular quarter. The end-of-quarter proctored examination will be conducted is mostly held in online mode.

    Grades: Prior to 2022 Fourth Quarter, Seven letter grades: A*, A, B, C, D, E, F can be awarded. The correspondence between grades and points (on a 10-point scale) is as follows:
    A*: 10, A: 10, B: 8. C: 6, D: 4, E: 2. F: 0

    Grades: From 2022 Fourth Quarter, Ten letter grades: A*, A, B+, B, C+, C, D+, D, E, F can be awarded. The correspondence between grades and points (on a 10-point scale) is as follows:
    A*: 10, A:10, B+:9, B:8, C+:7, C:6, D+:5, D:4, E:0, F:0

    In every registered module, a student is awarded one of the letter grades indicating his/her overall performance in the module and earns 5 credits except when the awarded grade is E or F in which case no credits are earned. If a student does not complete sufficient requirements for a module, the instructor may award grade I (Incomplete). The student must complete the module requirements within 2 quarters (in 4 quarters for the programme takes admission once in a year), after which the instructor will convert an I grade to a regular letter grade, failing which it will be automatically converted to an F grade.

    A student getting a D or D+ grade in a module may be allowed to repeat it or substitute it with another module as suggested by the academic authorities. A student getting a D grade in a module may be allowed to repeat it.

    The minimum CPI requirement for continuing in the program or for graduation will be 6.
